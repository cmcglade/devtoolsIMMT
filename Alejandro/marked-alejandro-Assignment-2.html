Alejandro Anaya, Imported from Mexico

I came to Canada in 2003 after many happy years in Mexico running the family business. The drug cartel industry is one of the most the successful in the world and I was sent to Canada to grow sales and increase market share. This was a very important initiative because we knew that Canadians were looking for a way to escape their problems and find looking true happiness - something that Mexicans had already because of tequila.


Unfortunately, it turned out that Canadians were growing their own supply of marijuana which was better than what Mexico had to offer. I was forced to resign from my duties and live a normal life. In the process of exploring the Canadian drug world I fell in love with a beautiful Canadian woman and settled in Canada as a new resident.


What my family in Mexico thought about that:

  • who is this girl?
  • is it always cold in Canada?
  • why is he washing dishes for $6 dollar an hour?
  • do they have penguins as pets?
  • if it is so cold, how come they can grow marijuana?

In the end, I learned that stereotypes can be misleading and so I have decided to to avoid them.
